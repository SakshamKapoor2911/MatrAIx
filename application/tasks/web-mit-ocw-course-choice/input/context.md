# Task context

You have decided to begin one self-directed course through MIT
OpenCourseWare. You do not have a predetermined subject or a fixed shortlist.

Browse the public catalog at https://ocw.mit.edu/search/ as yourself. Let your
background, existing knowledge, interests, goals, preferred difficulty and
depth, and learning preferences shape the decision. A course can be a good
choice because it builds on what you already know, helps you enter a new area,
supports a personal or professional goal, or presents material in a format that
works well for you.

MIT OCW courses differ in how much information and which resource types they
publish. Use only information visible on the live site, and do not assume that
every course states prerequisites or weekly time requirements.
