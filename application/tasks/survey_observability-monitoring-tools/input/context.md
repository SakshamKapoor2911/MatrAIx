# Observability and Monitoring Tools

How engineering teams monitor and observe their software systems, and their attitudes toward the tooling.

## About this survey

This survey profiles how teams approach observability and monitoring, opening with a screener on frequency, tenure, role, and primary platform. It records the platforms used, telemetry collected, instrumentation and tracing tools, dashboarding and alerting integrations, observability maturity, pillar coverage, OpenTelemetry adoption, alert routing, and what teams monitor. Attitudes are captured with an agreement Likert battery on root-cause speed, alert noise, dashboard trust, and cost, and a per-item importance matrix over what matters when choosing a tool, plus frequency scales for false positives and dashboard use during incidents. Additional sections cover SLOs, log volume handling, challenges, mean-time-to-resolution trends, a top-three ranking of priorities, platform-consolidation attitudes, satisfaction with the platform and alerting, recommendation likelihood, and vendor-change intent. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking item, and numeric free-text. The survey concludes with a demographic block covering role, professional experience, employment, company size, industry, age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, U.S. region, and urbanicity.
