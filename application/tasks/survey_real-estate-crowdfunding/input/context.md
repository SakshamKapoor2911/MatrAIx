# Real Estate Crowdfunding

How people invest in real estate through crowdfunding platforms and how they judge the deals and returns.

## About this survey

This survey profiles participation in real estate crowdfunding, opening with a screener on usage status, tenure, and broader real estate background before mapping behaviors: the platforms used, deal types invested in, primary goals, and how respondents first learned about the space. Attitudes are captured through an agreement Likert battery on diversification, returns, risk understanding, and fees, a concern scale over illiquidity, market downturns, platform failure, and interest rates, and a per-item importance matrix on deal-selection criteria, plus a top-three ranking of decision factors. Additional sections drill into amounts invested, deal counts, realized returns, portfolio share, accreditation status, reinvestment, adverse experiences, taxes, and satisfaction with returns and transparency, followed by recommendation likelihood and forward-looking intent. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, agreement and importance matrices, a ranking item, and numeric free-text. The survey ends with a demographic block covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urbanicity, and homeownership status.
