# Prompt Engineering Practices

How people write, test, and refine prompts for large language models, including techniques, tools, and attitudes.

## About this survey

This survey profiles prompt-engineering practice, opening with a screener on frequency, tenure, and usage context. It records the primary LLM used along with the models, tasks, techniques, and tooling respondents rely on, how they iterate when prompts fail, iteration counts, template reuse, system-prompt use, evaluation methods, and whether they have built LLM applications, plus numeric questions on daily prompt volume and first-try success rate. Attitudes are captured with an agreement Likert battery on skill value, output quality, future relevance, predictability, and reliability concerns, and a per-item importance matrix over prompt-crafting elements, along with frequency scales on chain-of-thought use and output editing and a top-three ranking of effective techniques. Later sections measure satisfaction with output quality, tooling, self-rated confidence, and overall experience, followed by recommendation likelihood on a 0–10 scale, learning and tool-adoption intent, and how respondents keep up with best practices. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert agreement, importance, frequency, satisfaction, confidence and likelihood scales, two matrix batteries, a ranking item, and numeric and open free-text. The survey closes with a demographic block covering role, professional experience, employment, company size, industry, programming ability, age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, U.S. region, and urbanicity.
