# Online Therapy Attitudes

Consumer awareness, preferences, and attitudes toward online and virtual therapy services.

## About this survey

This survey measures how consumers perceive online and virtual therapy, starting with a screener on familiarity, prior use, and number of sessions attended. It records specific behaviors and preferences including which services people have heard of and used, appealing session formats, reasons to consider online therapy, and how they first learned about it. Attitudes are captured through an agreement Likert battery on effectiveness, convenience, privacy, accessibility, and value, an importance matrix over service-selection factors such as cost, therapist qualifications, and confidentiality, and likelihood, comfort, and overall-attitude scales. Additional sections cover who online therapy suits, concerns, adoption triggers, payment expectations, insurance-coverage attitudes, how views have shifted, a top-three ranking of decision factors, a 0-to-10 recommend scale, and numeric free-text items on sessions attended, reasonable monthly cost, and preferred session length. The survey concludes with a demographic block covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urbanicity, insurance status, telehealth coverage, and comfort with technology.
