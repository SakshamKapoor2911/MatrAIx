# Digital Detox Habits

How people manage screen time and practice digital-detox habits, including methods and motivations.

## About this survey

This survey explores efforts to reduce screen time and practice digital detoxes, beginning with a screener on whether someone limits screen time, how concerned they are, and how much leisure screen time they have. It records specific behaviors including which devices and apps drive usage, detox methods tried, screen-time management tools used, and motivations for cutting back. Attitudes are captured with an agreement Likert battery on overuse, mood, self-control, and sleep, an importance matrix over factors that support reducing screen time such as goals, tools, and device-free times, plus frequency scales for phone-checking and screen breaks. Additional sections cover bedroom phone habits, app limits, benefits noticed, barriers, satisfaction and success of detox efforts, a top-three ranking of motivations, continuation and recommendation intent including a 0-to-10 recommend scale, and numeric free-text items on daily screen hours and phone checks. The survey ends with a demographic block covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urbanicity, insurance status, children in the household, and overall technology use.
