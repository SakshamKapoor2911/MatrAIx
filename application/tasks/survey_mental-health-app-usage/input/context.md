# Mental Health App Usage

How consumers adopt, use, and evaluate mental wellness and meditation apps.

## About this survey

This survey profiles the use of mental wellness and meditation apps, beginning with a screener on whether and how long a person has used one, how often, and which app they rely on most. It records specific behaviors including the apps and devices used, how people discovered their main app, the features and content types they engage with, session timing and length, and subscription and spending patterns. Attitudes are captured through an agreement Likert battery on stress management, routine fit, privacy, and value, an importance matrix over app-selection criteria such as cost, ease of use, and data protection, and satisfaction scales for content, usability, value, and overall experience. Additional sections cover goals, perceived benefits, barriers, mood tracking, use alongside a therapist or coach, a top-three ranking of reasons for use, likelihood and recommendation measures including a 0-to-10 recommend scale, and numeric free-text items on weekly usage, session length, and monthly spend. The survey closes with a comprehensive demographic block covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urbanicity, insurance status, and access to employer wellness benefits.
