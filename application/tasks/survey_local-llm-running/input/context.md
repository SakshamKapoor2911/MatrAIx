# Local LLM Running

How enthusiasts and developers run local and open large language models on their own hardware, including tools, models, and hardware constraints.

## About this survey

This survey profiles local LLM usage, opening with a screener on frequency, tenure, and main motivation. It records the primary runtime along with the tools, open model families, and tasks respondents use, their hardware type, GPU, memory budget, quantization level, largest model size, where they source models, fine-tuning practices, interface preference, and additional capabilities they have set up, plus numeric questions on models tried and disk usage. Attitudes are captured with an agreement Likert battery on privacy, cloud replacement, setup complexity, cost, and hardware limits, and a per-item importance matrix over model-selection criteria, along with frequency scales on hitting hardware limits and trying new models and a top-three ranking of reasons for running locally. Later sections measure satisfaction with quality, speed, and the overall setup, followed by recommendation likelihood on a 0–10 scale, hardware-upgrade and continued-use intent, and a comparison against cloud LLMs. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert agreement, importance, frequency and satisfaction scales, two matrix batteries, a ranking item, and numeric and open free-text. The survey closes with a demographic block covering role, professional experience, employment, company size, industry, a hardware-hobby item, age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, U.S. region, and urbanicity.
