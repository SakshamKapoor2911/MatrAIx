# Code Review Practices

How developers and teams conduct code review, including the tools, turnaround, standards, and attitudes involved.

## About this survey

This survey profiles code-review practices, opening with a screener on frequency, tenure, role, and the respondent's relationship to review. It records the primary review platform and captures process through multi-selects covering platforms used, supporting tools, required CI checks, and review focus areas, plus single-choice items on pull-request size, turnaround, required reviewers, templates, AI-assisted review, and comment resolution. Attitudes are measured with an agreement matrix about the respondent's team review culture and an importance matrix over what makes a review good, along with varied Likert items on review-caused delays, AI-review value, large-PR quality, and time well spent, a top-three effectiveness ranking, and a pain-point multi-select. Later sections capture satisfaction with the process and turnaround, a 0–10 recommendation scale, and AI-adoption and process-change intent, with numeric free-text on reviews per week and hours to first review and an optional open comment. The survey closes with a demographic block covering role, professional experience, employment, company size, industry, age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, U.S. region, and urbanicity.
