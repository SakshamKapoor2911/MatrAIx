# Pair Programming Habits

How developers practice pair and mob programming, the tools they use, and their attitudes toward it.

## About this survey

This survey profiles how developers pair and mob program, opening with a screener on frequency, tenure, role, session style, and remote versus in-person setup. It records the collaboration tools respondents use, the situations in which they pair, the benefits they have experienced, the languages they pair in, session length, and how driver and navigator roles rotate. Attitudes are captured with an agreement Likert battery on code quality, bug catching, onboarding, and enjoyment, and a per-item importance matrix over what matters most during a session, plus frequency scales for disagreements and breaks. Additional sections cover barriers, dedicated pairing time, remote latency, debugging value, a top-three ranking of reasons to pair, satisfaction with tools and culture, recommendation likelihood, and future intent. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking item, and numeric free-text. The survey concludes with a demographic block covering role, professional experience, employment, company size, industry, age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, U.S. region, and urbanicity.
