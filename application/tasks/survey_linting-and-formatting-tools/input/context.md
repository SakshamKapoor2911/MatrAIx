# Linting and Formatting Tools

How developers adopt, configure, and feel about code linters and formatters.

## About this survey

This survey profiles how developers use linters and formatters, opening with a screener on frequency, tenure, role, and the primary linter and formatter in use. It records the specific tools used across languages, how they are run (on save, pre-commit, CI, or manually), pre-commit tooling, configuration approach, CI enforcement, format-on-save, and editor integrations. Attitudes are captured with an agreement Likert battery on bug catching, style debates, config maintenance, and warning noise, and a per-item importance matrix over what matters when choosing a tool, plus frequency scales for disabling rules and blocked pull requests. Additional sections cover all-in-one tools, config strictness, pain points, a top-three ranking of formatter qualities, satisfaction with the linter and formatter, code-quality impact, recommendation likelihood, and switching intent. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking item, and numeric free-text. The survey concludes with a demographic block covering role, professional experience, employment, company size, industry, age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, U.S. region, and urbanicity.
