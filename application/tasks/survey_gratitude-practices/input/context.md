# Gratitude Practices

How people practice gratitude, including methods, frequency, and perceived benefits.

## About this survey

This survey explores personal gratitude practices, opening with a screener on whether someone practices gratitude, how long they have, and how often. It records specific behaviors including the methods and apps used, when and with whom gratitude is shared, how people got started, and their primary goal. Attitudes are captured with an agreement Likert battery on positivity, relationships, routine, and stress, an importance matrix over factors that sustain a gratitude practice such as daily habit, writing things down, and specificity, plus frequency scales and satisfaction and meaningfulness ratings. Additional sections cover benefits noticed, barriers, consistency, reminder use, a top-three ranking of reasons for practicing, continuation and recommendation intent including a 0-to-10 recommend scale, and numeric free-text items on weekly practice days, items reflected on per session, and thank-you notes sent. The survey ends with a demographic block covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urbanicity, insurance status, self-rated overall wellbeing, and the importance of religion or spirituality.
