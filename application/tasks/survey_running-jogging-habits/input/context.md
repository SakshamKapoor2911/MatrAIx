# Running and Jogging Habits

How people run and jog for fitness, including their frequency, gear, and tracking apps.

## About this survey

This survey profiles runners and joggers, starting with a screener on whether and how long they have run, how often, and their main motivation. It records where and when they run, running-shoe brands, tracking apps such as Strava, Nike Run Club, and Garmin Connect, devices and gear used, shoe-replacement habits, race participation, and numeric items on weekly mileage and recent shoe spend. Attitudes are captured through an agreement Likert battery on lifestyle, tracking motivation, shoes, and stress relief, and an importance matrix over shoe-buying criteria, plus a top-three ranking of shoe factors and a multi-select of useful app features. Additional sections cover social sharing, injury frequency, complementary activities, and satisfaction with routine, app, shoes, and progress, followed by a recommendation-likelihood scale and intent to race, increase volume, or keep running. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking item, and numeric free-text. The survey concludes with a demographic block covering fitness level, age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, and urbanicity.
