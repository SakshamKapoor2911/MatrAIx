# Farmers Market Shopping

How people shop at farmers markets for fresh and local food, including their frequency and motivations.

## About this survey

This survey profiles farmers market shoppers, starting with a screener on shopping frequency, tenure, seasons visited, and travel distance. It records what shoppers buy, their motivations, how they get to market, payment methods, other local-food channels used, and numeric items on per-visit spend and number of vendors. Attitudes are captured through an agreement Likert battery on freshness, local support, cost, and environmental value, an importance matrix over where-to-buy criteria, and a barrier matrix rating price, hours, distance, selection, and cash, plus a top-three ranking of reasons for choosing farmers markets. Additional sections cover the share of produce from markets, cooking follow-through, and satisfaction with quality, prices, and variety, followed by an overall satisfaction rating, a recommendation-likelihood scale, and intent to shop more or join a CSA. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, two matrix batteries, a ranking item, and numeric free-text. The survey concludes with a demographic block covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, and urbanicity.
