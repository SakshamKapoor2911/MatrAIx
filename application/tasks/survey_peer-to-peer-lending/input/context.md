# Peer-to-Peer Lending

How people participate in peer-to-peer lending platforms as investors, borrowers, or both.

## About this survey

This survey profiles experience with peer-to-peer lending, opening with a screener on usage status, tenure, and primary role before mapping behaviors: the platforms used, loan types involved, how respondents first heard about P2P lending, and how investors select loans to fund. Attitudes are captured through an agreement Likert battery on returns, risk, disclosure, and trust, a concern scale over default, platform failure, liquidity, and downturns, and a per-item importance matrix on lending and borrowing criteria, plus a top-three ranking of decision factors. Additional sections drill into invested and borrowed amounts, realized returns and rates, portfolio share, adverse experiences, reinvestment, monitoring cadence, taxes, and satisfaction with returns, tools, and support, followed by recommendation likelihood and forward-looking intent. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, agreement and importance matrices, a ranking item, and numeric free-text. The survey concludes with a demographic block covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urbanicity, and investment risk tolerance.
