# Secrets Management Tools

How developers and teams store, protect, and manage application secrets and credentials.

## About this survey

This survey profiles how teams manage secrets, opening with a screener on frequency, tenure, role, and primary tool. It records the secrets tools used, kinds of secrets managed, how secrets are injected into applications, local-development storage, rotation practices, security practices followed, secret scanning, and how CI/CD accesses secrets. Attitudes are captured with an agreement Likert battery on protection, sprawl, pain, and confidence that no secrets are committed, and a per-item importance matrix over what matters when choosing a tool, plus frequency scales for accidental exposure and credential rotation. Additional sections cover .env usage, secret sharing, pain points, dynamic secrets, a top-three ranking of priorities, attitudes about short-lived credentials, satisfaction with the tool and developer experience, recommendation likelihood, and tool-change intent. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking item, and numeric free-text. The survey concludes with a demographic block covering role, professional experience, employment, company size, industry, age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, U.S. region, and urbanicity.
