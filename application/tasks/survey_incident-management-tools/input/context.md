# Incident Management Tools

How engineering teams handle on-call and incident response, including tooling, practices, and postmortems.

## About this survey

This survey profiles how teams manage on-call and incident response, opening with a screener on rotation membership, tenure, role, and primary tool. It records the incident tools used, where teams coordinate during incidents, what triggers a page, schedule structure, on-call frequency, severity levels, postmortem practices, and incident roles. Attitudes are captured with an agreement Likert battery on on-call load, alert actionability, process clarity, and stress, and a per-item importance matrix over what matters when evaluating an incident tool, plus frequency scales for off-hours paging and postmortem coverage. Additional sections cover on-call compensation, acknowledgment targets, pain points, runbooks, a top-three ranking of what makes response effective, blameless-culture attitudes, satisfaction with tooling and the on-call experience, recommendation likelihood, and tool-change intent. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking item, and numeric free-text. The survey concludes with a demographic block covering role, professional experience, employment, company size, industry, age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, U.S. region, and urbanicity.
