# Sinking Funds

How people set aside money in dedicated sinking funds for planned, irregular future expenses.

## About this survey

This survey profiles how respondents plan and save for irregular expenses using sinking funds, opening with a screener on whether they use them, for how long, and how many goals they maintain. It records which expense categories they save for, where the money is held, the apps and tools they rely on such as Ally buckets, Capital One 360, Chime, YNAB, Monarch Money, and Qapital, how they decide contribution amounts, and whether contributions are automated. Attitudes are captured with an agreement Likert battery on debt avoidance, motivation, temptation, and stress, plus a per-item importance matrix over savings-account features, a top-three ranking of reasons for using sinking funds, and a multi-select of challenges. Additional sections cover fund raiding, review cadence, emergency funds, and outcomes including satisfaction, goal confidence, recommendation likelihood, and switching intent. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, varied Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking item, and numeric free-text. The survey closes with a demographic block covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urbanicity, and homeownership.
