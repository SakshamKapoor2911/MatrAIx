# Crowdfunding Backing

Why and how people back crowdfunding campaigns and how they evaluate the projects they support.

## About this survey

This survey profiles crowdfunding backing behavior, opening with a screener on backing history, tenure, and recent activity before mapping specifics: the platforms and campaign types backed, the main motivations, and how respondents discover projects. Attitudes are captured through an agreement Likert battery on donation-versus-purchase framing, trust, and fees, a concern scale over non-delivery, scams, delays, and spending, and a per-item importance matrix on campaign-evaluation criteria, plus a top-three ranking of decision factors. It also uses frequency scales on altruistic backing, reading updates, and sharing, and drills into contribution amounts, adverse experiences, launching a campaign, delay reactions, and satisfaction with rewards, platforms, and creator communication, followed by recommendation likelihood and forward-looking intent. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, agreement and importance matrices, a ranking item, and numeric free-text. The survey concludes with a demographic block covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urbanicity, and general charitable-giving frequency.
