# Personal Bankruptcy Attitudes

Public attitudes toward personal bankruptcy, the stigma surrounding it, and respondents' own experience considering or filing Chapter 7 or Chapter 13.

## About this survey

This survey opens by screening for personal experience with bankruptcy, familiarity with Chapter 7 versus Chapter 13, and current debt stress. It gathers specifics for those who filed or considered filing, including triggers, professionals consulted, chapter chosen, costs, and alternatives tried. The core of the instrument measures attitudes and stigma through an agreement matrix battery and an importance matrix battery, alongside questions on judgment of others, fairness of the system, and knowledge of how long bankruptcy stays on a credit report. Outcomes and intentions are captured with satisfaction, likelihood, a 0-to-10 recommendation score, and confidence measures. Question types span single-choice, multi-select, varied Likert scales, a top-3 ranking, and numeric free-text, and the survey concludes with demographics including age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, community type, homeownership, health insurance status, and family bankruptcy history.
