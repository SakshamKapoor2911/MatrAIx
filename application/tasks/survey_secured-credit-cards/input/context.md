# Secured Credit Card

How people use secured credit cards to build or rebuild credit, including deposits, issuers, card usage, and the path to an unsecured card.

## About this survey

This survey screens for secured card ownership and the cardholder's credit goal, then captures the issuer, security deposit, credit limit, annual fee, application channel, feature priorities, bureau reporting, and monthly usage. It measures decision drivers through an importance battery (fee, deposit, reporting, graduation path, rewards, APR) and attitudes through an agreement battery on credit-building progress, understanding, and payment discipline, plus a top-3 ranking and a payment-frequency item. Drill-down sections cover credit monitoring, credit-building steps, score change, graduation status, and current score range, followed by satisfaction, plans for the card, and a 0-10 likelihood-to-recommend scale. Numeric free-text captures the deposit amount and current credit score, with an optional open comment. A comprehensive demographics block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, community type, banking status, and number of credit cards.
