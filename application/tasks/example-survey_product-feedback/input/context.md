# FocusLoop — product concept brief

**Category:** Family productivity / household coordination  
**Stage:** Concept test (not yet launched)

## One-liner

FocusLoop helps busy parents keep school, activities, and shared errands visible
in one calm weekly view — without another group chat.

## Problem

Dual-income households juggle school notices, medical appointments, and travel
in scattered channels (WeChat, email, paper flyers). Missed deadlines create
stress and last-minute scrambling.

## Proposed solution

- **Shared family calendar** with color-coded members (parents + kids)
- **Weekly digest** every Sunday evening: what matters next week
- **Lightweight task handoffs** (“pick up package”, “sign permission slip”) with
  one-tap accept
- **Optional school email parsing** (beta) to auto-suggest events — user reviews
  before adding

## Pricing (proposed)

- **Free:** 2 members, manual events only
- **Plus ($6.99/mo):** up to 6 members, digest, handoffs
- **Pro ($11.99/mo):** email parsing, shared grocery list
- 7-day trial on Plus or Pro; cancel anytime
- Annual billing saves about 35% vs monthly
- Occasional promos (for example, first month at $1 on Plus)

## Target user

Urban professionals aged 28–45 with at least one school-age child, already using
digital calendars but frustrated by fragmentation.

## What this survey is probing

- Default pay intent after trying free
- Plus vs Pro receptivity
- Annual vs monthly billing
- Promo reaction
- Switching behavior vs calendar + messaging status quo
- Ads on free vs paying to remove them
- Overall interest and pricing fairness
