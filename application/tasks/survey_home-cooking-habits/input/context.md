# Home Cooking Habits

How people cook at home and pursue healthy eating, including their methods, tools, and barriers.

## About this survey

This survey profiles home cooks, starting with a screener on how often they cook, who does the cooking, and how they typically get dinner. It records cooking methods and appliances, recipe sources, meal-planning and recipe apps, meal-kit use, planning and batch-cooking frequency, healthy-eating practices, and numeric items on weekly home-cooked dinners and grocery spend. Attitudes are captured through an agreement Likert battery on enjoyment, cost, confidence, and time, a barrier matrix rating time, cost, skills, cleanup, and household preferences, and an importance matrix over meal-choice criteria, plus a top-three ranking of barriers. Additional sections cover takeout frequency, vegetable use, self-rated skill, and satisfaction with variety and healthiness, followed by an overall satisfaction rating, a recommendation-likelihood scale, and intent to cook more and eat healthier. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, two matrix batteries, a ranking item, and numeric free-text. The survey concludes with a demographic block covering eating patterns, age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, children in the household, U.S. region, and urbanicity.
