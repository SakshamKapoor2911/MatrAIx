# Financial Independence, Retire Early (FIRE)

Attitudes, savings behavior, and targets among people engaging with the Financial Independence, Retire Early movement.

## About this survey

This survey profiles attitudes toward the FIRE movement, opening with a screener on familiarity, relationship to FIRE, tenure, and preferred flavor before mapping behaviors: current savings rate, target retirement age, motivations, strategies, and accounts used. Attitudes are captured through an agreement Likert battery on sacrifice, priorities, frugality, confidence, and relationships, a concern scale over longevity, market downturns, healthcare, inflation, and purpose, and a per-item importance matrix on FIRE-plan levers, plus a top-three ranking of levers. Additional sections drill into the FIRE number, expected spending, progress, withdrawal rate, resources followed, tracking cadence, post-FIRE work plans, obstacles, happiness effects, and satisfaction with progress and savings rate, followed by recommendation likelihood and forward-looking intent. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, agreement and importance matrices, a ranking item, and numeric free-text. The survey ends with a demographic block covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, dependents, U.S. region, and urbanicity.
