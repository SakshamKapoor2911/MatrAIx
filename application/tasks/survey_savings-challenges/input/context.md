# Savings Challenges

How people use structured savings challenges such as the 52-week challenge and no-spend months to save money.

## About this survey

This survey explores participation in structured savings challenges, beginning with a screener on whether respondents have tried one, how long ago, and how many. It records which challenges they have attempted, their motivations, the apps and tools they use such as Qapital, Chime, Acorns, and paper trackers, how they fund the savings, and which spending categories they cut during no-spend periods. Attitudes are measured with an agreement Likert battery on fun, consistency, motivation, and habit change, plus a per-item importance matrix over success factors, a top-three ranking of benefits, and multi-selects of inspiration sources and abandonment obstacles. Further sections cover completion frequency, no-spend cadence, accountability, and outcomes including satisfaction, confidence, recommendation likelihood, and future intent. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, varied Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking item, and numeric free-text. The survey ends with a demographic block covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, and urbanicity.
