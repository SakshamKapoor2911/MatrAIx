# Testing Framework Usage

How developers choose and use automated testing frameworks and manage test coverage across languages.

## About this survey

This survey profiles automated-testing practices, opening with a screener on frequency, tenure, role, and the type of testing done most. It captures tooling through multi-selects covering JavaScript, end-to-end, Python, and other-language frameworks, kinds of tests written, mocking and assertion libraries, and coverage tools, plus single-choice items on the primary framework, where tests run, coverage targets, and test-driven development. Attitudes are measured with an agreement matrix about the respondent's primary framework and an importance matrix over selection criteria, along with varied Likert items on TDD frequency, coverage value, Vitest as a Jest replacement, and end-to-end flakiness, a top-three factor ranking, and a challenges multi-select. Later sections capture satisfaction with the framework and suite speed, a 0–10 recommendation scale, and adoption and switching intent, with numeric free-text on coverage targets and suite size and an optional open comment. The survey closes with a demographic block covering role, professional experience, employment, company size, industry, age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, U.S. region, and urbanicity.
