# Decentralized Finance (DeFi)

How people adopt, use, and evaluate decentralized finance protocols for lending, trading, and yield.

## About this survey

This survey profiles engagement with decentralized finance, opening with a screener on usage status, tenure, and frequency before mapping specific behaviors: the blockchains, decentralized exchanges, lending protocols, wallets, and stablecoins respondents use, plus the DeFi activities they perform and how they research new protocols. Attitudes are captured through an agreement Likert battery on returns, trust, fees, and complexity, a concern scale over key DeFi risks, and a per-item importance matrix on protocol-selection criteria, along with a top-three ranking of decision factors. Further sections drill into allocation size, realized yields, loss experiences, monitoring habits, portfolio tracking, taxes, and satisfaction with yields, security, and the overall experience, followed by recommendation likelihood and forward-looking intent. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, importance and agreement matrices, a ranking item, and numeric free-text. The survey closes with a demographic block covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urbanicity, and investor risk tolerance.
