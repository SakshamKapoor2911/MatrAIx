# Workplace Financial Wellness

How employees learn about, use, and value the financial wellness benefits and programs their employers provide.

## About this survey

This survey profiles employee experience with workplace financial wellness benefits, opening with a screener on what the employer offers, tenure, and the specific benefits and providers available such as retirement plans, financial coaching, student loan assistance, emergency savings programs, and platforms including Fidelity, Empower, SoFi at Work, and Financial Finesse. It records how respondents learned about benefits, which they have used, usage frequency, retirement participation and contribution rate, financial stress, its effect on work, and the topics they most want help with. Attitudes are captured with an agreement Likert battery on employer care, usefulness, confidence, and privacy, and a per-item importance matrix over specific benefit types, plus a top-three ranking of most valuable benefits, a multi-select of barriers to use, and a recommendation likelihood scale. Additional sections cover emergency savings, fit with needs, outcomes, outside guidance, and satisfaction with benefits, communication, and overall financial situation. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, agreement and importance matrices, satisfaction and likelihood Likert scales, an NPS-style 0–10 item, a ranking item, and numeric free-text. The survey closes with a demographic block covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, household income, education, employment, employer size, industry, household size, marital status, U.S. region, and urbanicity.
