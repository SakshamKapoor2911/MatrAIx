# Data Visualization Tools

How analysts, scientists, and developers create data visualizations and choose among BI platforms and charting libraries.

## About this survey

This survey profiles data visualization practice, opening with a screener on frequency, tenure, and primary reason for creating visualizations. It records the main tool along with the BI platforms, charting libraries, chart types, data sources, audiences, and output channels respondents work with, their build approach, design-principle adherence, color-selection method, and accessibility considerations, plus numeric questions on monthly output and weekly hours. Attitudes are captured with an agreement Likert battery on decision-making impact, chart-type confidence, code versus GUI control, time cost, and organizational value, and a per-item importance matrix over tool-selection criteria, along with frequency scales on interactive-chart use and stakeholder change requests and a top-three ranking of decision factors. Later sections cover challenges faced, satisfaction with ease of use, customization, and the tool overall, followed by recommendation likelihood on a 0–10 scale and tool-adoption and skill-learning intent. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert agreement, importance, frequency and satisfaction scales, two matrix batteries, a ranking item, and numeric and open free-text. The survey closes with a demographic block covering role, professional experience, employment, company size, industry, self-rated visualization skill, age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, U.S. region, and urbanicity.
