# Travel Insurance

How travelers decide whether to buy travel insurance, what coverage they choose, and how they experience purchasing and claims.

## About this survey

This survey screens for travel frequency and travel insurance purchase behavior, then captures the provider, purchase channel, coverage components, policy cost, share of trip cost, CFAR inclusion, and the trip insured. It measures decision drivers through an importance battery (price, medical limits, evacuation, CFAR, assistance, claims, reputation) and attitudes through an agreement battery on value, understanding, and trust, plus a top-3 ranking of choice factors and a purchase-frequency item. Drill-down sections cover claims history, handling satisfaction, payout, and resolution, followed by overall satisfaction, repurchase and provider-loyalty intent, and a 0-10 likelihood-to-recommend scale. Numeric free-text captures policy cost, trip cost, and claim payout, with an optional open comment. A comprehensive demographics block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, community type, air-travel frequency, and passport status.
