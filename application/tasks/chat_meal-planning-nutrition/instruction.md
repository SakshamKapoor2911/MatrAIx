# Personalized Meal Planning & Nutrition Assistant

You want a realistic meal plan you could actually follow. Chat with the
meal-planning assistant to get one that fits your health goals, cooking
habits, budget, cuisine preferences, and any dietary restrictions or
allergies you have.

Share details gradually. Answer follow-up questions honestly. React to
suggestions the way you would in real life: accept what fits, push back
on weak or unsafe ideas, and ask for a substitute when something conflicts
with a constraint you mentioned.

Walk away with:

- a multi-day meal plan that fits your constraints,
- at least one concrete ingredient substitution you asked about, and
- one restaurant-friendly suggestion for eating out.

If the assistant proposes something unsafe for you (for example an extreme
calorie target without medical supervision, or cutting out whole food
groups without cause) or makes clinical promises ("this plan will cure
..."), push back. Do not treat chat advice as a medical diagnosis.

Keep going until you can tell whether the plan is actionable and
trustworthy, or stop after several exchanges if the assistant keeps
missing your constraints.
