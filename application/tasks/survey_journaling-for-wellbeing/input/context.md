# Journaling for Wellbeing

How people use journaling to support their wellbeing, including tools, frequency, and perceived benefits.

## About this survey

This survey profiles journaling as a wellbeing practice, opening with a screener on whether someone journals, how long they have, how often, and typical session length. It records specific behaviors including journaling methods, apps used, styles practiced, timing, how people got started, and their primary goal. Attitudes are captured with an agreement Likert battery on stress, emotional understanding, routine, and privacy, an importance matrix over factors that sustain a journaling habit such as privacy, ease, prompts, and consistency, plus frequency scales and satisfaction ratings for the habit and the tool used. Additional sections cover prompt use, re-reading entries, benefits noticed, barriers, consistency, complementary wellbeing practices, a top-three ranking of reasons for journaling, continuation and recommendation intent including a 0-to-10 recommend scale, and numeric free-text items on weekly journaling days, session length, and entries written. The survey concludes with a demographic block covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urbanicity, insurance status, and self-rated overall wellbeing.
