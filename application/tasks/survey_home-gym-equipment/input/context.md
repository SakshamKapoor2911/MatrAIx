# Home Gym Equipment

How people own, use, and feel about home gym and fitness equipment.

## About this survey

This survey profiles consumer home-fitness behavior, opening with a screener on whether respondents have equipment, how long they have worked out at home, how often they use it, and where it is set up. It records the specific equipment, cardio machines, strength gear, and brands owned (including Peloton, Bowflex, NordicTrack, Rogue, Concept2, Tonal, and Hydrow), how it was acquired, connected-fitness subscriptions, apps used, workout types, spending, and floor space. Attitudes are captured with an agreement Likert battery on usage, value, and motivation and a per-item importance matrix over purchase criteria such as price, space, durability, app features, brand, and resale value, plus a top-three ranking of reasons to work out at home and multi-selects for barriers and tracking methods. Later sections cover reliance on guided classes, idle-equipment frequency, whether a commercial membership is also kept, satisfaction with quality and value, recommendation likelihood, and future purchase and subscription intent. The survey closes with a demographic block covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urbanicity, and homeownership.
