# Protein Supplement Usage

How people use protein powders, shakes, and bars, including their brands, goals, and attitudes.

## About this survey

This survey profiles consumers of protein supplements, beginning with a screener on whether and how long they have used protein products and how often they consume them. It records the forms and protein types used, brands purchased, shopping channels, goals, timing of consumption, flavors, and preparation methods, along with numeric items on monthly spend and daily protein targets. Attitudes are captured through an agreement Likert battery on the role of supplements, label reading, plant-based protein, and sweeteners, plus a per-item importance matrix over purchase criteria and a top-three ranking of buying factors. Additional sections cover digestive tolerance, dietary restrictions, companion supplements, and satisfaction with taste, value, and dietary fit, followed by an overall satisfaction rating, a recommendation-likelihood scale, and intent to try, switch, or continue. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking item, and numeric free-text. The survey concludes with a demographic block covering activity level, age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urbanicity, and gym membership.
