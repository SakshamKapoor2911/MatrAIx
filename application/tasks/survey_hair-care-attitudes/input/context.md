# Hair Care Attitudes

How people care for their hair, including routines, products, styling methods, brands, and attitudes.

## About this survey

This survey profiles hair care behavior and attitudes, opening with a screener on wash frequency, hair type, main concern, and the products used. It records specific brands, styling methods, coloring and chemical treatment, where cuts and products are obtained, and time spent on hair. Attitudes are captured with an agreement Likert battery on self-presentation, willingness to pay, and product-finding struggles, a matrix battery on what respondents value in a hair product, and a per-item importance matrix over purchase criteria, plus a top-three ranking of buying factors. Additional sections cover heat styling, novelty seeking, information sources, at-home scalp devices, and satisfaction with shampoo, styling results, and the overall routine, followed by recommendation likelihood and intent to change routine or brands. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, varied Likert scales, two matrix batteries, a ranking item, an NPS-style 0-to-10 scale, and numeric and open free-text. The survey concludes with a demographic block covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, and urbanicity.
