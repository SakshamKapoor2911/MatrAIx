# Travel Savings and Budgeting

How people save, budget, and use rewards to fund their travel and vacations.

## About this survey

This survey examines how respondents plan and finance leisure travel, opening with a screener on whether and how they save for trips and how often they travel. It records the trip types and expenses they budget for, where travel savings are held, the apps and tools they use such as Ally and Capital One buckets, YNAB, Hopper, Going, and travel-card portals, how they fund and how far ahead they plan, and how they book. Attitudes are captured with an agreement Likert battery on guilt-free spending, rewards, overspending, and prioritization, plus a per-item importance matrix over trip-planning factors, a top-three ranking of saving priorities, and multi-selects of money-saving strategies, rewards programs, and overspending triggers. Additional sections cover rewards-card usage, in-trip tracking, travel debt, and outcomes including satisfaction, confidence, recommendation likelihood, and future intent. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, varied Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking item, and numeric free-text. The survey concludes with a demographic block covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, children at home, U.S. region, and urbanicity.
