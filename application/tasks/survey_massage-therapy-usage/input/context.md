# Massage Therapy

How people use and feel about massage therapy for wellness, relaxation, and recovery.

## About this survey

This survey profiles consumer massage-therapy behavior, opening with a screener on whether and how often respondents receive professional massages and when they last went. It records the massage types received and preferred, settings used (day spas, franchises such as Massage Envy, clinics, independent studios, and mobile therapists), session length, price paid, number of sessions per year, payment methods, membership status, reasons for going, and at-home tools such as percussion guns and foam rollers. Attitudes are captured with an agreement Likert battery on wellness value and perceived benefits and a per-item importance matrix over provider-selection criteria such as therapist skill, price, convenience, atmosphere, reviews, and online booking, plus a top-three ranking of sought benefits and a barriers multi-select. Later sections cover pressure preference, therapist loyalty, booking method, at-home tool frequency, post-massage relaxation, satisfaction with quality and value, recommendation likelihood, and future usage intent. The survey closes with a demographic block covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urbanicity, and physical activity level.
